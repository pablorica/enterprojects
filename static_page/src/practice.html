People

Patrick Keane
Director

Patrick Keane, a seventh generation Australian who has lived significantly in five different continents, has allowed his global travels to translate back into the workplace.

He uses his experiences and knowledge to introduce forward thinking approaches in Australia by using new technology in a creative environment to explore space and form. Patrick achieved his Bachelor of Environmental Design Degree at the University of Western Australia before setting his sights on the University of Princeton USA, graduating with a Masters in Architecture.

In the latter, Patrick played an intrinsic role in the internationally published, luxury Fraser development where he designed and modelled the geometrical arcs that were used on all facades of the 33 storey residential suite tower. Rene-Paul van Leeuwen
Design Manager

Rene-Paul is a Designer with extensive design and detailing skills across a wide range of projects. He has degrees in Technical Engineering, Architecture, Urbanism and Building Sciences which gives him a unique understanding of the relationship between design and detail. He uses extensive software and 3D modelling programs including Rhinoceros, 3D Studio Max, AutoCAD, VRay Render and Grasshopper.

Whilst still in his homeland of the Netherlands, Rene worked with the Government Building Agency in the departments of Urban Planning, Architecture and Project Management. Working on large scale projects for five years a change occurred when he decided to relocate to Australia where he designed Indigenous Architecture in Arnhem Land, The Northern Territory, in collaboration with UTS Sydney.

Residing permanently in Sydney he has worked as a Design Manager and Project Architect on approximately 80 projects; ranging from master-planning, high-rise, residential, commercial and interior.
